**UNIVERSAL RULES (apply to ALL genres):**
- The video must feel like a narrator-driven story. Visuals should support and illustrate what the narrator is saying with **URGENCY AND PACE**.
- Use native audio generation and dialogue delivery in the video prompt when supported by the model.
- Write prompts so spoken lines are clearly produced and character mouth movement matches speech (lip-sync).
- Instead of generic audio cues, specify exact spoken dialogue lines and who says them.
- Dialogue format example inside prompts: Person 1: "Hey, how are you?" Person 2: "Fine."
- Characters must actively talk to each other with clear spoken dialogue — but dialogue must happen DURING the incident, not as a static conversation. Stage lines mid-confrontation, mid-standoff, mid-chase, mid-revelation.
- **NO STATIC DIALOGUE SCENES**: Never show two characters standing still and talking. If dialogue is happening, something is also happening around them — a standoff, a pursuit, a moment of crisis, an intense face-to-face under pressure.
- Dialogue and action are simultaneous: the character speaks WHILE the scene is in motion. Use close-ups on the speaker's face during a dynamic moment, then cut back to the wider kinetic scene.
- Focus on strong visual storytelling plus speech-driven moments: expressive scenes, dramatic imagery, text overlays, and cinematic compositions.
- For rapid dialogue: Use quick cuts between characters, close-ups on expressions, dynamic camera angles
- For emotional moments: Use dramatic camera movements, not static shots
- For action/revelation: Use speed lines, impactful compositions, dramatic lighting
- If narration includes dialogue (quoted lines), represent it on screen using visible dialogue text (e.g., speech bubbles, lower-third quote cards, or subtitles tied to the speaker).
- Do not drop dialogue lines: key quoted lines from narration must appear as on-screen text instructions in videoPrompt.
- For dialogue shots, include explicit lip-sync guidance in videoPrompt, such as: "speaker faces camera, mouth movements accurately synchronized to the spoken line".
- **MUSIC DIRECTION**: Visuals should match "fast-paced, energetic background score" - create scenes that feel urgent and engaging.
