GENRE CLASS — Identify this before shortlisting anything.

Genres fall into two classes. The class determines what "worth researching further" actually means.

**CLASS 1 — TIMELESS GENRES**
Mythology, Horror, Fantasy, Folklore, Martial Arts, Legend, Sci-Fi, Romance, Tragedy, Supernatural

For Timeless genres "worth researching further" means:
- There is rich, original source material available — foundational epics, folk legends, classic texts, world-famous stories — that can be turned into a compelling short-form video.
- The genre has proven, enduring audience appeal that does not depend on a recent release.
- A Timeless genre is worth shortlisting if it has great stories in it, full stop. The year is irrelevant.

What does NOT make a Timeless genre worth shortlisting:
- A video game released recently happens to use that genre's characters (e.g., Black Myth: Wukong does NOT make Mythology suddenly worth picking — Mythology was always worth picking because Journey to the West exists).
- A Netflix or streaming title released this year happens to be in that genre.
- "Trending" search results for the genre that all point to a recent product or adaptation.

If a recent game, movie, or streaming release appears as a reason to shortlist a Timeless genre, treat it only as a SIGNAL that the audience appetite for the original source material is strong right now. The genre is shortlisted because the original stories are compelling, not because of the product.

**CLASS 2 — RECENCY-APPROPRIATE GENRES**
News, Current Events, Technology, Science, Politics, Economics, Business, Sports (recent), Health

For Recency genres "worth researching further" means:
- There is a genuinely timely, relevant, and narratively compelling story happening right now.
- Current events, recent discoveries, and trending topics with strong human-interest angles are legitimate here.
- Recency and momentum ARE appropriate signals for these genres.
