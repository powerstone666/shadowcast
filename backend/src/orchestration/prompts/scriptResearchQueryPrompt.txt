GENRE CLASS — Identify this before generating a single query.

Genres fall into two classes. The class determines what your queries must target.

**CLASS 1 — TIMELESS GENRES**
Mythology, Horror, Supernatural, Fantasy, Biography, History

For Timeless genres the script must be built from ORIGINAL SOURCE MATERIAL — the foundational text, epic, scripture, folk legend, or classic story that the topic actually comes from. Your three queries must dig into that original source, not into any recent adaptation of it.

SOURCE MATERIAL HIERARCHY for Timeless Genres (apply strictly):
1. Original canonical texts and scriptures — the actual epic, novel, scripture, or foundational story.
   - Wukong topic → Journey to the West (16th century Chinese novel by Wu Cheng'en). NOT Black Myth: Wukong (2024 video game).
   - Greek mythology topic → Homer's Iliad/Odyssey, Hesiod's Theogony. NOT a 2025 or 2026 movie.
   - Horror topic → the originating folk legend, oral tradition, or world-famous classic story. NOT a Netflix series from any recent year.
   - Dracula → Bram Stoker's original novel and Wallachian folklore. NOT a recent adaptation.
2. Classic folklore and oral tradition — stories passed down for generations across cultures.
3. World-famous, most-loved versions of the story — the ones that have endured because they are genuinely compelling.
4. Historical and scholarly analysis of the story — what experts say about its meaning, structure, and cultural significance.

WHAT TO AVOID IN QUERIES FOR TIMELESS GENRES (NEVER generate these):
- Any query that names a specific video game as the research target: "Black Myth Wukong gameplay story" → WRONG
- Any query that targets a specific streaming release: "Netflix horror series 2026 plot" → WRONG
- Any query with "trending [year]", "best [genre] movies 2026", "latest [genre] game" → WRONG
- Any query that researches a product or adaptation instead of the foundational story → WRONG

If the topic was inspired by a recent game or movie, that is irrelevant to the research. The script must be built from the original story. Ignore the adaptation entirely.

QUERY STRUCTURE FOR TIMELESS GENRES — generate exactly three queries in this order:

Query 1 — THE ORIGINAL SOURCE
Target the foundational text, epic, or folk legend directly. Find the actual story as it was originally told.
Examples:
- "Sun Wukong Bull Demon King battle Journey to the West Wu Cheng'en original story"
- "La Llorona origin Latin American folklore legend original version"
- "Medusa myth original Greek mythology Ovid Perseus story"
- "Dracula Bram Stoker original novel story Vlad Tepes folklore"
- "Hercules twelve labors original Greek myth Lernaean Hydra details"

Query 2 — THE STORY DETAILS AND KEY EVENTS
Dig into the specific narrative elements needed to write a compelling script: character abilities, turning points, what happened beat by beat, the dramatic climax, the emotional stakes.
Examples:
- "Sun Wukong powers abilities staff transformations Journey to the West chapters details"
- "La Llorona story full narrative what happened to her children versions"
- "Medusa Perseus fight sequence how Perseus defeated Medusa mythology details"
- "Bull Demon King powers weakness how Wukong defeated him story arc"
- "Hercules Hydra fight how he won strategy Iolaus role original myth"

Query 3 — CULTURAL WEIGHT AND WHY IT RESONATES
Find what makes this story universally compelling: its emotional core, its cultural significance, why people have retold it for centuries, what the story means.
Examples:
- "Journey to the West Sun Wukong cultural significance why Chinese mythology endures"
- "La Llorona why this horror legend has terrified generations cultural meaning"
- "Medusa myth meaning feminist interpretation original story analysis"
- "most retold horror folk legends what makes them terrifying to audiences"
- "why Sun Wukong is one of the most beloved mythological heroes in the world"

**CLASS 2 — RECENCY-APPROPRIATE GENRES**
Geopolitics, Technology, Science, Sports

For these genres, the queries should target current, accurate, and detailed information about the specific topic. Use all three queries to get different angles: what happened, why it matters, and what the human story behind it is.

QUERY STRUCTURE FOR RECENCY GENRES:
Query 1 — What specifically happened: the core event, facts, key figures, and timeline.
Query 2 — The deeper context: why it happened, background, stakes involved, cause and effect.
Query 3 — The human angle: personal stories, reactions, consequences felt by real people, what changed.
