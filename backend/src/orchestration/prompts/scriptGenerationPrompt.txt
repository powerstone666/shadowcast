**SCROLL OPTIMIZATION - THE 1-3 SECOND HOOK:**
- **IMMEDIATE ATTENTION GRAB**: The first 1-3 seconds MUST stop viewers from scrolling.
- **FIRST LINE RULE**: First spoken line should be shocking, surprising, contradictory, or create intense curiosity.
- **HOOK EXAMPLES**: 
  - "Everything you know about [topic] is wrong."
  - "This wasn't a curse, it was armor."
  - "They said [common belief], but the truth is [counterpoint]."
  - "What if I told you [surprising revelation]?"
- **VISUAL HOOK**: First visual should be dramatic, emotional, or visually striking to complement the audio hook.
- **NO SETUP**: Don't start with context or setup. Start with the punchline, revelation, or conflict.

**MID-VIDEO RETENTION - THE 45-60 SECOND MARK:**
- **MID-SECTION ENERGY**: The middle of the script (approximately 45-60 seconds in) must maintain or increase energy.
- **AVOID MID-SECTION DROP**: Common drop-off point - ensure visual storytelling remains as dynamic as the opening.
- **MID-POINT REVELATION**: Place a significant revelation, twist, or emotional peak around the 45-60 second mark.
- **TIGHTEN MIDDLE SECTIONS**: If story feels slow in middle, cut to the "reveal" faster.
- **MID-SECTION PACING TECHNIQUES**:
  - Introduce new conflict or complication
  - Reveal hidden motivation or backstory
  - Heighten emotional stakes
  - Quicken dialogue pace
  - Add visual variety (change location, perspective, or visual style)
- **ENERGY FLOW**: Hook (0-3s) → Setup (3-30s) → Build-up (30-45s) → **REVELATION (45-60s)** → Resolution (60-90s)

YouTube Shorts quality requirements:
- **FIRST 1-2 LINES = SCROLL STOPPER**: Must work within 1-3 seconds of video start.
- **RAPID PACING**: No pauses between lines, no silent moments, no dead air. Keep momentum constantly high.
- Every section should add new value to the story - cut anything that doesn't advance plot or character.
- Focus on one clear narrative thread with concrete stakes centered around a specific incident.
- Include moments that are easy to visualize and emotionally engaging with Anime-style animation.
- **STRONG CONCLUSION**: End with a proper conclusion that provides:
  - **EMOTIONAL PAYOFF**: Resolve the emotional journey started in the hook
  - **CLEAR RESOLUTION**: Answer the central question or conflict posed at beginning
  - **MEMORABLE CLOSING LINE**: Leave viewer with a thought-provoking or inspiring final statement
  - **SATISFYING COMPLETION**: Story should feel complete, not abrupt or unfinished
  - **CALL TO REFLECTION**: End with line that makes viewer think about the story's meaning
- **CONCLUSION TECHNIQUES**:
  - Reveal the moral or lesson learned
  - Connect back to the opening hook
  - Provide emotional closure for characters
  - End with powerful dialogue or narrator reflection
  - Leave audience with "aha" moment

Story requirements:
- Provide a practical short-form script or structured outline suitable for voiceover.
- **FAST PACING**: Keep dialogue rapid-fire. Characters should speak immediately after each other, no pauses.
- **INCIDENT-EMBEDDED DIALOGUE**: Dialogue must happen DURING the incident or confrontation — not as a separate conversation scene. Characters speak while the event is unfolding around them. Think: sharp lines traded mid-standoff, mid-chase, mid-revelation, mid-conflict — not two people standing still and talking.
- **KINETIC DIALOGUE**: Every dialogue exchange must have a clear action context. Where are they? What is happening at that exact moment? What forces them to speak?
- **ENGAGING DIALOGUE**: Lines should sound urgent, emotional, and high-stakes — like words forced out under pressure, not scripted conversation.
- Include at least 3-4 short dialogue exchanges embedded within active scenes or turning points.
- Dialogue format example (with action context):
  [Mid-confrontation, both characters facing off]
  Person 1: "You really think you can stop this?"
  Person 2: "I already have."
- Characters must actively talk to each other with clear spoken dialogue that reveals personality and drives the incident forward.
- Write dialogue with lip-sync in mind (clean, speakable lines that match visible mouth movement).
- **BACKGROUND MUSIC**: Mention only as "fast-paced, energetic background score" or "upbeat, driving soundtrack" - never "light ambient". Music should match the urgency of the scene.
- **NO NARRATOR PAUSES**: The narrator should speak continuously, flowing directly into dialogue without breaks.
- **ENERGY LEVEL**: Maintain high energy throughout. If tension drops, introduce new conflict or revelation immediately.
- LENGTH CONSTRAINT: The script MUST be timed to take 30 to 90 seconds to read aloud. NEVER exceed 120 seconds under any circumstance. Scripts approaching 120 seconds should maintain tight pacing. For narrative stories, 60-90 seconds allows for proper story development.
