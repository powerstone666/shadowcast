GENRE STORYTELLING MODE CHECK (MANDATORY):

The genre determines HOW the story must be told. Evaluate whether the script uses the CORRECT storytelling mode for its genre — this is not optional.

- **ACTION / FIGHT / MYTHOLOGY / FANTASY** (e.g., Wukong vs demon, god wars, epic battles):
  The script must read like a match commentator narrating a fight. Narration should describe physical events beat by beat with vivid action verbs. Character spoken lines must be SHORT explosive cries (2–6 words) dropped at peak moments — NOT full conversations, NOT monologues, NOT characters standing still explaining their feelings. A fight scene where characters are mainly having a dialogue exchange is a FAILURE of genre mode. Penalise it heavily.

- **HORROR / THRILLER / SUPERNATURAL / DARK**:
  The script must build escalating dread through narration. Structure: wrong detail → escalation → the reveal → consequence. Character lines must be short, fractured, scared — NOT a calm conversation about what is happening. Atmosphere must be the main character. A horror script where characters discuss the situation like a meeting is a FAILURE.

- **DRAMA / HISTORY / BIOGRAPHY**:
  Dialogue IS appropriate as the story driver here. 3–4 back-and-forth exchanges embedded at the story's critical moment are expected and should be evaluated for quality, urgency, and emotional weight.

- **DOCUMENTARY / SCIENCE / TECHNOLOGY / GEOPOLITICS**:
  Narrator-led revelation format. Evaluate the hook fact, the unpacking, and whether the twist lands. Quoted lines or reactions should be sparse but impactful. For Geopolitics, visuals must be grounded — never sci-fi.

When scoring, first identify which mode the genre demands, then evaluate whether the script actually delivers that mode. A drama script judged by fight-scene standards, or a fight scene judged by dialogue-exchange standards, would both be wrong.

---

Score it from 0 to 10, where:
- 10 means excellent and ready to publish
- 7 means publishable with only minor fixes
- below 7 means the script is not ready

Score using this rubric (each criterion contributes to your overall score):

- **Hook strength & Demographic appeal** (0–2 pts): Does the first 1–3 seconds grab attention instantly? Does it stop scrolling? Critically: Does it appeal to Gen Z and young adults (ages 13–24) and the massive Indian/Global market? Hooks must be punchy, high-energy, and instantly understandable. A high-scoring hook uses a massive curiosity gap, epic stakes, or mind-blowing facts. Boring, slow, or purely academic hooks must score 0.

- **Genre storytelling mode** (0–2 pts): Does the script use the CORRECT storytelling mode for the genre?
  - For ACTION/FIGHT: Is the script a vivid sequence of physical events with short battle cries? Or is it characters standing still in conversation? Sequence-driven = 2 pts. Conversation-driven for a fight scene = 0 pts.
  - For HORROR: Does narration build genuine dread stage by stage? Or is it a calm discussion? Atmosphere-driven = 2 pts. Calm conversation about the horror = 0 pts.
  - For DRAMA/HISTORY: Are 3–4 dialogue exchanges embedded at the critical moment? Are they urgent and emotionally weighted? Well-placed dialogue = 2 pts.
  - For DOCUMENTARY: Does narration reveal and unpack compellingly? Clear revelation structure = 2 pts.

- **Pacing & narrative flow** (0–2 pts): Is the energy consistently high? No dead air, no slow middle section? Most importantly: Does the script read like ONE continuous, flowing story? If it reads like choppy, disjointed bullet-point sentences randomly cut together, score it 0 and demand a rewrite for smooth narrative continuity.

- **Mid-video retention** (0–2 pts): Is there a clear revelation, twist, physical turning point, or emotional peak around the 45–60 second mark?

- **Conclusion quality** (0–2 pts): Does the script end with emotional payoff, a clear resolution, and a memorable closing line? Does it feel complete and not abrupt?
