GENRE CLASS — Read this before selecting anything.

Genres fall into two classes. The class determines HOW you pick the topic.

**CLASS 1 — TIMELESS GENRES**
Mythology, Horror, Fantasy, Folklore, Martial Arts, Legend, Sci-Fi, Romance, Tragedy, Supernatural

For these genres the BEST topic is NOT the most recent adaptation, game release, or streaming title.
It is the most compelling ORIGINAL SOURCE MATERIAL — the foundational story, epic, scripture, folk tale, or legend that the audience actually wants to see told.

SOURCE MATERIAL HIERARCHY for Timeless Genres (apply strictly in this order):
1. Original canonical texts and scriptures — the actual epic, novel, scripture, or foundational story.
   - Wukong → Journey to the West (16th century Chinese novel). NOT Black Myth: Wukong (2024 video game).
   - Greek mythology → Homer's Iliad/Odyssey, Hesiod's Theogony. NOT a 2026 movie adaptation.
   - Horror → the originating legend, folk tale, or world-famous classic. NOT a Netflix 2026 release.
2. Classic folklore and oral tradition — stories passed down for generations across cultures.
3. World-famous, most-loved stories in that genre — the ones that have endured because they are genuinely compelling.
   - Horror: The Tell-Tale Heart, Dracula, Frankenstein, Japanese Kuchisake-onna, Slenderman origin, The Banshee, La Llorona.
   - Mythology: Ramayana battles, Mahabharata turning points, Norse Ragnarok, Hercules labors, Medusa's origin.
   - Martial Arts/Legend: Sun Wukong's 72 transformations, The Water Margin heroes, legendary duel stories.
4. Classic adaptations with decades of proven cultural staying power.

WHAT TO TREAT AS EVIDENCE, NOT AS THE TOPIC:
- A recent video game (Black Myth: Wukong) appearing in search results = evidence that the original story (Journey to the West) is in strong cultural consciousness right now. USE THAT SIGNAL to pick the original story as the topic.
- A recent Netflix/streaming release in a timeless genre = evidence of audience appetite for that genre. Pick the foundational story or folk legend, NOT the Netflix show.
- A recent movie adaptation = evidence the myth is relevant. Tell the ORIGINAL myth, not the movie.

The topic for a Timeless Genre should ALWAYS be traceable to a real, foundational source — a text, a legend, a folk tale, a classic story. Never a product release.

**CLASS 2 — RECENCY-APPROPRIATE GENRES**
News, Current Events, Technology, Science, Politics, Economics, Business, Sports (recent), Health

For these genres, recency and trending momentum ARE appropriate primary signals. Current events, recent discoveries, and trending stories are legitimate topics. Use search results to find what is genuinely hot right now.

SELECTION RULES:

- Select exactly one genre to post next from the configured genres.
- First, identify which class the genre belongs to (Timeless or Recency-Appropriate).
- If a user preference is provided, strongly prioritize aligning the chosen genre, topic, and title to that preference.
- Avoid repeating recently overused genres unless the story angle is clearly different and stronger.
- Do not choose a topic already covered recently unless the new angle is clearly fresher and more compelling.
- Treat currentDate/currentYear as the timeline anchor for Recency genres. For Timeless genres, the year is irrelevant to topic choice — a great story from 200 BCE is still a great story.

FOR TIMELESS GENRES — topic selection:
- Ask: "What is the most famous, compelling, and emotionally rich story within this genre that the audience would want to see brought to life?"
- Prefer stories with a clear conflict, a dramatic turning point, a legendary character doing something extraordinary, or a folk horror premise that has terrified people for generations.
- A topic like "The fight where Sun Wukong defeated the Bull Demon King from Journey to the West" is CORRECT.
- A topic like "Black Myth: Wukong game story recap" is WRONG — it is a product, not a story.
- A topic like "Classic horror legends: the origin of La Llorona" is CORRECT.
- A topic like "Netflix horror movie review 2026" is WRONG — it is a product, not a story.

FOR RECENCY-APPROPRIATE GENRES — topic selection:
- Use current search signals as primary evidence.
- Pick what is genuinely relevant and trending right now with strong narrative potential.
- Frame through the human angle — decisions made, stakes faced, outcomes felt.
